Student Study: Sustainable Urban Development

Format Student Study
(group-based, student-led)
University Zeppelin University
Supervisor Chair of Socio-Economics
Collaboration City of Friedrichshafen
(municipal planning practice)
Case "Langes Feld" — Friedrichshafen's first climate-neutral urban district
Year 2021

Overview

Together with three fellow students, I organised the Student Study "Sustainable Urban Development" — a semester-long academic format at Zeppelin University. A Student Study allows students to design and run their own course, combining academic input with real-world planning practice.

The central case was the planned development of "Langes Feld", Friedrichshafen's first climate-neutral urban district. The area is a long-term urban development project that confronts central tensions in contemporary planning: How do you design for climate goals while remaining socially inclusive? How do you balance expert knowledge with citizen participation?

Visual summary of the Langes Feld district planning outcomes (in German)

Interdisciplinary Process & Methods

The Student Study combined expert input, methodological training and collaborative group work in an iterative process. We invited practitioners from the City of Friedrichshafen and the consultancy City&Bits to contribute lectures, case input and feedback sessions on the "Langes Feld" district.

Participants worked in small groups on specific dimensions of sustainable urban development for the Langes Feld case. Thematic focus areas included:

  • Mobility and transport infrastructure
  • Energy systems and climate adaptation
  • Social mix and affordable housing
  • Green and open space planning
  • Digital infrastructure and smart city
  • Stakeholder participation and governance

Methodologically, the course relied on stakeholder analysis, long-term perspective building and user-centred design approaches, including personas developed in collaboration with City&Bits. A strong emphasis was placed on recognising real planning trade-offs — for example between climate targets, cost efficiency and social equity.

Outcome

The Student Study resulted in five coherent conceptual approaches, each addressing a distinct dimension of sustainable urban development for the Langes Feld site. Key outcomes included:

  • Integrated scenario plans combining mobility, energy and social design
  • A stakeholder map identifying key actors and their interests in the development
  • A consolidated sketch combining the outputs of all groups into a shared spatial vision

Beyond the concrete outputs, the Student Study functioned as a real-world learning laboratory — developing skills in self-organisation, interdisciplinary collaboration and the art of communicating planning proposals to non-academic audiences.

Personal Learning

Organising the course was my first direct exposure to planning actors from municipalities, academia and the private sector working on a shared urban challenge. It confirmed my interest in the local scale as a site where global sustainability goals become concrete, contested and actionable.

Through the Student Study, I also established contact with the consultancy City&Bits, who contributed a lecture on personas, Design Thinking and their work with municipalities. This connection later led to my internship there, where I worked on Smart City projects for German cities.
